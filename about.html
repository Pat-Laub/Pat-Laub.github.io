About Me

Hi! I’m Pat Laub, a mathematician & software engineer currently finishing a PhD in applied probability (with a focus on computational methods). My PhD jointly between Aarhus University and University of Queensland, and I’m lucky to have as supervisors Søren Asmussen and Phil Pollett. I’m interested complex systems, and the intersection of maths/stats and computing, for example: computational and experimental maths, machine learning, statistics (MCMC), economics, artificial intelligence. I like to read about biology (mainly genetics), history, and international relations. I have worked in software roles, at Google & Data61 (then called NICTA), and have taught programming and probability at my universities over 8 years. For more background you can check out my LinkedIn profile.
