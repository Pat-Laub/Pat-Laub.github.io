Patrick Laub

Mathematician & software engineer

Hi, my name’s Patrick Laub. I’m a Lecturer at UNSW in the School of Risk and Actuarial Studies. I teach courses on deep learning and on statistical machine learning for actuaries. My PhD was in applied probability with a focus on computational methods, and it was jointly conducted between Aarhus University and University of Queensland. I was lucky to have as supervisors Søren Asmussen and Phil Pollett. I’m interested in the intersection of maths/stats and computing in actuarial science.

I have worked at the University of Melbourne, at ISFA in the Université Claude Bernard Lyon 1 (Lyon, France), at Google (Sydney) & Data61 (then called National ICT Australia). I have taught programming and probability at universities since 2009. For more background you can check out my LinkedIn profile or my publications below.

  • Creator of the Julia package EMpht.jl which fits phase-type distributions
